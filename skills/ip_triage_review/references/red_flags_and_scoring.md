# IP Red Flags And Scoring

Use this reference when rating IP triage risk.

## High-Priority Red Flags

- Patent list provided without claim charts or claim scope explanation.
- Key patent family is pending, narrow, near expiry, or not owned by licensor.
- Composition of matter protection is absent or weak for a product where it is expected.
- Background IP agreement is missing or limits sublicensing, territory, field, assignment, or change of control.
- FTO analysis is missing in a crowded mechanism, modality, delivery, or manufacturing space.
- Royalty stack is undisclosed.
- Inventorship, assignment, university rights, government funding rights, or employee obligations are unclear.
- Ongoing opposition, IPR, litigation, or third-party challenge exists.
- Patent term assumptions ignore prosecution status, PTE/SPC limits, or regional differences.
- Exclusivity claim relies on method-of-use only while commercial use may be broader.

## IP Triage Scoring

| Score | Meaning | Typical Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 3 | Strong triage position | Claim charts, ownership, expiry, FTO, and encumbrances are clear enough for business review |
| 2 | Manageable uncertainty | Gaps exist but are defined and counsel can review before signing |
| 1 | Material risk | Exclusivity, ownership, FTO, or encumbrance issue could affect value or rights |
| 0 | Not assessable | Core IP documents are missing |

## Conclusion Rules

- `Supportive`: Strong or manageable IP position, subject to counsel confirmation.
- `Supportive With Conditions`: Proceed only with counsel review, reps, warranties, or conditions.
- `Mixed`: Asset may be attractive but exclusivity or encumbrance risk affects economics.
- `Not Supportive`: IP issue appears likely to block or severely impair transaction value.
- `Not Assessable`: Missing claim charts, FTO, ownership, or key agreements.

## Required Counsel Questions

- Which claims protect the product, indication, formulation, manufacturing, and use?
- Who owns or controls each patent family?
- Are sublicensing and regional rights sufficient for the proposed deal?
- What third-party IP could block development or commercialization?
- What royalty stack or consent obligations reduce value?
