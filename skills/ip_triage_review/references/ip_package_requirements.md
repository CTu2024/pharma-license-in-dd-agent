# IP Package Requirements

Use this reference when checking whether IP diligence materials are complete enough for counsel and valuation.

## Core Documents

Request and review:

- Patent family list with application numbers, countries, status, expiry, and owners.
- Claim charts for composition, method of use, formulation, manufacturing, biomarkers, combinations, or platform claims.
- Prosecution history and office action summary for key families.
- Assignment and chain-of-title records.
- Existing license agreements, background IP agreements, university or inventor obligations.
- FTO, patentability, validity, non-infringement, or landscape opinions if available.
- Opposition, IPR, litigation, settlement, or challenge history.
- Regulatory exclusivity analysis: data, orphan, pediatric, biologic, or regional equivalents.
- Royalty stack, sublicensing rights, field, territory, and consent requirements.

## Asset-Type Focus

### Small Molecule

- Composition of matter claim strength and expiry.
- Polymorph, salt, formulation, method-of-use, and manufacturing claims.
- Patent term extension potential.

### Biologic

- Composition, sequence, epitope, formulation, method, manufacturing, and device claims.
- BPCIA or biosimilar-relevant exclusivity issues.
- Platform or background IP constraints.

### Cell / Gene / RNA / Complex Modalities

- Vector, construct, sequence, delivery, manufacturing, promoter, editing, and process claims.
- Platform ownership and improvement rights.
- Third-party foundational IP and sublicensing rights.

## Output Expectations

For each material issue, state:

- What is known.
- What is missing.
- Why it matters commercially.
- What counsel must review.
- Whether valuation should assume base, downside, or unresolved exclusivity.
