# CDA Gate Question Library

Use this reference when deciding whether a sparse opportunity should move to CDA/confidential diligence.

## Must Answer Before CDA

These questions determine whether CDA is worth the time, confidentiality exposure, and internal attention.

### Strategic Fit

- Which internal strategy does this opportunity serve?
- Which therapeutic area owner would sponsor the next step?
- Is the stage aligned with our risk appetite?
- Are we interested in the relevant modality and development geography?
- Would this asset complement, compete with, or distract from our portfolio?

### Reason To Believe

- What is the single strongest evidence point?
- What is the weakest link in the opportunity story?
- Which claims are based on primary data versus management interpretation?
- What has been independently validated by competitors, publications, or clinical precedent?
- What would make the opportunity still interesting if the strongest claim weakens?

### Lead Indication Logic

- What is the proposed lead indication and why?
- What alternatives were considered and deprioritized?
- Is the lead chosen for biology, speed, market size, regulatory feasibility, investor appeal, or partner preference?
- What is the first clinically meaningful proof-of-concept endpoint?
- What result would kill the program?

### Transaction Actionability

- What rights are available: global, regional, field, indication, option, co-development, co-commercialization, or acquisition?
- Is the company seeking capital, capabilities, geography, validation, or exit?
- Is there a competitive process and timeline?
- Are valuation expectations broadly realistic for stage and evidence maturity?
- Are any rights unavailable because of existing obligations?

### Data Existence

- Which full reports exist today?
- Which reports are final, draft, ongoing, or planned?
- Which data will be available immediately after CDA?
- Which deck claims do not yet have final reports behind them?
- Is the data room ready or still being assembled?

## Can Answer After CDA

- Full pharmacology, PK, tox, CMC, regulatory, IP, and clinical report details.
- Raw data, statistical outputs, and protocol-level methods.
- Patent claim charts, FTO analysis, and legal agreements.
- Forecast model, pricing assumptions, and detailed deal economics.
- Detailed manufacturing process, CDMO, batch, and release materials.

## Internal Alignment Needed

- Who owns the opportunity internally?
- What minimum evidence is required before economics?
- What risk level is acceptable for this stage?
- What transaction structure would fit our appetite?
- Who should attend the intro call?
- What would be our walk-away threshold?
