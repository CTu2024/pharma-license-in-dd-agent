# Scouting Score And Action Rules

Use this reference to convert a high-level opportunity review into a clear BD next action.

## Scoring Dimensions

Use `High / Medium / Low / Unknown`.

- Strategic fit.
- Scientific rationale.
- Differentiation potential.
- Lead indication clarity.
- Development feasibility.
- Competitive attractiveness.
- Evidence maturity.
- Transaction actionability.
- Internal ownership.
- Urgency / scarcity.

## Action Rules

### Pass

Use when:

- Strategic fit is low.
- Opportunity is outside mandate.
- No credible reason-to-believe is visible.
- Transaction rights are incompatible.
- Seller expectations are clearly unrealistic.

### Monitor

Use when:

- Opportunity is interesting but too early.
- Key inflection is near but not yet available.
- Internal fit is unclear.
- No immediate process pressure exists.

### Intro Call

Use when:

- Opportunity is plausible but basic facts are unclear.
- A short call can resolve lead indication, data existence, transaction fit, or seller motivation.
- Internal time cost is low.

### Request Non-Confidential Follow-Up

Use when:

- Deck is interesting but key claims need non-confidential clarification.
- CDA would be premature without knowing what reports exist.
- Need a data inventory, lead indication rationale, IP summary, or process overview.

### Proceed To CDA / Confidential Diligence

Use when:

- Strategic fit is at least medium.
- Reason-to-believe is credible.
- Lead indication and next milestone are clear.
- Seller confirms full reports exist and will be available.
- Transaction rights appear potentially compatible.
- Internal owner exists.

## Scouting Kill Signals

- No primary reports exist for core claims.
- Lead indication is still vague after follow-up.
- Claimed differentiation rests on a single fragile preclinical point.
- Transaction rights are not available or unclear.
- Seller refuses to clarify what data exist after CDA.
- No internal owner is willing to sponsor the next step.
