# Target Validation And Translational Red Flags

Use this reference when deciding whether target biology should support a CDA.

## Red Flags

- Target is described as broadly relevant to many diseases without lead indication discipline.
- Evidence is mostly expression data without causal or functional validation.
- Animal model efficacy is presented as proof of human disease relevance.
- Biomarker is not linked to target engagement, pathway modulation, or clinical outcome.
- Competitor validation is in a different indication, modality, population, or endpoint.
- Safety expression pattern raises concerns but is treated as clean.
- Biology supports chronic use but safety evidence is short-term only.
- Mechanism overlaps with immunosuppression or fibrosis biology without clear patient selection.

## Confidence Rating

- `High`: Human disease biology, competitor/clinical validation, biomarker logic, and modality fit align.
- `Medium`: Plausible rationale with partial external validation but limited direct evidence.
- `Low`: Mostly animal, expression, or pathway-level rationale.
- `Not assessable`: Key target evidence is missing or unsourced.

## CDA Gate Questions

- What is the strongest human evidence supporting this target in the lead indication?
- Which competitor or clinical data validate the pathway?
- What biomarker confirms target engagement or pathway modulation?
- What biology would predict non-response or safety risk?
- What evidence supports indication prioritization?
