# Early CDA Target Screen

Use this reference when a target biology claim is being evaluated from non-confidential or high-level materials.

## Core Questions Before CDA

- Is the target/pathway linked to human disease biology?
- Is there competitor or clinical precedent validating the target?
- Is the claimed mechanism causal, correlative, or speculative?
- Does the mechanism support the proposed lead indication?
- Is there a biomarker or patient-selection logic?
- Is the modality suitable for the target location, biology, and dosing needs?
- What target-related safety risk is visible from biology, expression, genetics, or class precedent?

## Evidence Hierarchy

Higher confidence:

- Human genetics with disease relevance.
- Human clinical target validation.
- Human tissue/cell expression tied to disease lesions.
- Competitor clinical data in same pathway.
- Translational biomarker data linked to pharmacology.

Lower confidence:

- Animal model efficacy alone.
- Broad pathway diagrams.
- Expression without disease specificity.
- Company interpretation of literature without source detail.
- Biomarker movement without clinical or mechanistic linkage.

## Scouting Output

State:

- Why the target is interesting.
- What is externally validated.
- What is inferred.
- Which indication has strongest target rationale.
- What target evidence must be reviewed after CDA.
