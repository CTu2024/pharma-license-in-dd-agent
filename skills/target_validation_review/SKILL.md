# Target Validation Review Skill

## Use When

Assessing whether the target and mechanism are strong enough to support continued diligence.

## Review Method

1. State the target, disease, modality, and claimed mechanism.
2. Separate human evidence, nonclinical evidence, translational evidence, and company inference.
3. Check whether evidence supports disease relevance, causal role, pathway modulation, and patient selection.
4. Identify class, pathway, redundancy, resistance, safety, and biomarker risks.
5. Rate confidence and define what evidence would change the view.

## Output Fields

- Target and mechanism claim
- Evidence type: human / nonclinical / translational / inferred
- Source IDs
- Confidence: high / medium / low
- Key limitations
- Diligence questions

## References

- Read `references/early_cda_target_screen.md` when reviewing target biology from non-confidential or high-level materials before CDA.
- Read `references/translational_red_flags.md` when rating target validation confidence or writing target-biology CDA gate questions.

## Quality Boundary

Do not treat animal efficacy, pathway plausibility, or biomarker movement as human proof unless clinical or genetic evidence supports that inference.
