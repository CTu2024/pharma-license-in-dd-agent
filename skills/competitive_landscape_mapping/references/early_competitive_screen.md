# Early Competitive Screen

Use this reference when evaluating competitive attractiveness from non-confidential materials.

## Core Questions

- What is the current standard of care in the proposed lead indication?
- What is the nearest competitor by target, mechanism, modality, and stage?
- What competitor readouts will occur before this asset reaches PoC?
- What must be true for the asset to win?
- Is differentiation clinical, mechanistic, dosing, safety, access, CMC, or IP based?
- Which differentiation claim can be tested earliest?

## Scouting Evidence Needed

- Competitor name, mechanism, phase, indication, owner, timing.
- Current SoC and main unmet need.
- Target product profile.
- Evidence-to-claim map for differentiation.
- Expected launch or PoC timing compared with competitors.

## Red Flags

- "First-in-class" or "best-in-class" claim without competitor map.
- Differentiation based on animal data while competitor has human data.
- Convenience claim without PK/formulation feasibility.
- Lead indication chosen where competitors are much more advanced.
- SoC comparison uses weak or non-standard comparator.
