# Competitive Landscape Mapping Skill

## Use When

Assessing whether an asset's differentiation is credible against current and future treatment options.

## Review Method

1. Define disease setting, geography, line of therapy, patient segment, and expected launch timing.
2. Map current standard of care and approved competitors by mechanism, route, dosing, efficacy, safety, monitoring, access, and label.
3. Map relevant pipeline competitors by phase, mechanism, expected timing, likely positioning, and probability of disrupting the market.
4. Compare the asset's target product profile against current and future alternatives.
5. Identify which differentiation claims are evidence-supported, assumption-based, or not assessable.
6. Translate competitive threats into clinical, regulatory, market access, pricing, forecast, and deal-structure implications.

## Output Fields

- Competitor or standard-of-care item
- Relevance to target population
- Differentiation dimension
- Asset advantage / parity / disadvantage / unknown
- Evidence source
- Pipeline timing risk
- Decision implication
- Follow-up request

## Differentiation Rating

- `Credible`: Supported by clinical or label-relevant evidence against meaningful comparators.
- `Potential`: Plausible but dependent on incomplete evidence, future data, or indirect comparisons.
- `Weak`: Advantage is small, unsupported, commercially irrelevant, or easily matched.
- `Not assessable`: Required comparator, endpoint, label, or pipeline evidence is missing.

## Quality Boundary

Do not treat convenience, mechanism novelty, or company positioning as differentiation unless it is linked to evidence that matters to clinicians, patients, payers, regulators, or launch uptake.
