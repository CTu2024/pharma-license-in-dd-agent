# Clinical Evidence Red Flags And Scoring

Use this reference when writing the clinical evidence workstream conclusion.

## High-Priority Red Flags

- Only investor deck, poster, press release, or synopsis is available for a material clinical claim.
- Primary endpoint failed but selected secondary, subgroup, or post hoc results are emphasized.
- Comparator is placebo when standard of care or active comparator would be decision-relevant.
- Baseline imbalance favors the asset.
- High discontinuation, differential dropout, or missing data is unexplained.
- Safety summary lacks exposure duration, dose relationship, narratives, or AESI detail.
- Follow-up is too short for claimed durability or chronic safety.
- Study population does not match proposed label, line of therapy, or target product profile.
- Reported benefit is statistically significant but clinically marginal.
- Efficacy and safety claims depend on different doses or populations.

## Evidence Strength Scoring

Score each major clinical claim:

| Score | Meaning | Typical Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 3 | Strong | Primary documents, appropriate design, clinically meaningful endpoint, consistent benefit, adequate safety |
| 2 | Moderate | Directional signal with limitations, incomplete documents, small sample, short follow-up, or indirect comparator |
| 1 | Weak | Selected summaries, exploratory/post hoc evidence, weak design, limited safety exposure |
| 0 | Not assessable | Missing key source documents or analyses |

## Workstream Conclusion Rules

- `Supportive`: Most material claims score 3 and no unresolved high-severity safety issue exists.
- `Supportive With Conditions`: Core signal is credible, but specific gaps must be resolved before next milestone.
- `Mixed`: Evidence is directionally promising but important claims score 1-2 or material documents are missing.
- `Not Supportive`: Efficacy, safety, or benefit-risk evidence does not support the claimed product profile.
- `Not Assessable`: Key documents are missing and the workstream cannot judge the claim.

## Required Clinical Requests

When evidence is incomplete, ask for:

- CSR, protocol, amendments, SAP.
- Tables, figures, listings, datasets.
- Patient disposition, baseline characteristics, protocol deviations.
- AE/SAE/AESI/death narratives and exposure-adjusted safety tables.
- Endpoint definitions, analysis populations, missing data handling.
- Subgroup, durability, sensitivity, and comparator analyses.
