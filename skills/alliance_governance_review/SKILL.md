# Alliance Governance Review Skill

## Use When

Assessing whether a proposed license, collaboration, co-development, or co-commercialization partnership can be governed and operated effectively.

## Review Method

1. Identify the deal structure, parties, territories, development responsibilities, commercial responsibilities, and retained rights.
2. Map proposed governance bodies: joint steering committee, development, regulatory, CMC, commercial, finance, publication, and dispute forums.
3. Review decision rights, reserved matters, tie-breaks, escalation path, deadlock resolution, budget approvals, and plan changes.
4. Check operational interfaces: data sharing, reporting cadence, audit rights, vendor oversight, publication review, regulatory communications, safety reporting, and supply coordination.
5. Identify incentive misalignment, control gaps, unclear accountability, or issues that should become deal terms.
6. Translate alliance risks into negotiation points, transition needs, and post-signing operating model actions.

## Output Fields

- Governance issue
- Affected function
- Proposed mechanism or missing mechanism
- Decision-right concern
- Operating risk
- Deal-term implication
- Follow-up request

## Governance Readiness Rating

- `Ready`: Decision rights, escalation, responsibilities, and operating interfaces are clear.
- `Ready with conditions`: Gaps exist but can be resolved in term-sheet or definitive agreement drafting.
- `At risk`: Governance ambiguity could impair development, regulatory, CMC, commercial, or budget execution.
- `Not assessable`: Proposed governance and responsibilities are missing or too vague to judge.

## Quality Boundary

Do not assume a partnership is operable because economics are attractive. Governance quality must be assessed through decision rights, accountability, escalation, information flow, and operating responsibilities.
