# Risk Matrix Writing Skill

Use this skill to write risks in a consistent, decision-ready format.

## Risk Statement Pattern

Write each risk as:

`Because [evidence or gap], [specific adverse outcome] may occur, which could affect [decision, value, timeline, label, approval, launch, or operations].`

## Scoring

Severity:

- `High`: Could materially change deal decision, valuation, approval path, launch potential, or safety profile.
- `Medium`: Could change plan, terms, timeline, or mitigation budget.
- `Low`: Manageable with routine diligence or execution planning.

Likelihood:

- `High`: Supported by direct evidence or repeated pattern.
- `Medium`: Plausible but not proven.
- `Low`: Theoretical or currently weakly supported.

## Required Fields

- Risk ID
- Workstream
- Risk statement
- Evidence or source ID
- Severity
- Likelihood
- Decision impact
- Mitigation or next action
- Owner
- Status

## Writing Rules

- Avoid vague risks such as "CMC risk" or "clinical uncertainty."
- Make the adverse outcome explicit.
- Link each material risk to evidence, a data gap, or an assumption.
- Do not overstate precision when evidence is preliminary.
