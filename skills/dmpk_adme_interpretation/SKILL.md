# DMPK / ADME Interpretation Skill

## Use When

Dose, exposure, metabolism, DDI, or translation uncertainty may affect development risk.

## Review Method

1. Summarize available PK and ADME evidence by species and human data.
2. Identify clearance route, metabolite profile, CYP and transporter liabilities.
3. Check exposure consistency across dose levels, species, and formulation.
4. Compare exposure to pharmacology, tox, and clinical observations when available.
5. Flag missing DDI, food effect, renal, hepatic, or special population work.

## Output Fields

- Key PK / ADME finding
- Evidence source
- Development implication
- Missing data
- Risk level
- Specific company request

## References

- Read `references/biologic_pk_ada_checklist.md` when reviewing antibody or biologic PK, ADA, FcRn, half-life, or first-in-human dose assumptions.
- Read `references/dose_convenience_claim_test.md` when the opportunity claims long-acting dosing, Q4W/Q8W/Q12W/Q3M convenience, or better exposure as differentiation.

## Quality Boundary

Do not infer safety margin or dose adequacy from PK alone. Link exposure to pharmacology, toxicology, and clinical context.
