# Dose Convenience Claim Test

Use this reference when an opportunity claims long-acting, infrequent dosing, better exposure, or convenience differentiation.

## What Must Be True

- Exposure remains above the pharmacologically active threshold for the proposed interval.
- Inter-subject variability is manageable.
- ADA does not shorten exposure or compromise safety/efficacy.
- Formulation and dose volume are feasible for the route.
- Safety margin supports the dose and interval.
- Target biology does not require higher or more frequent pathway coverage.
- Comparator dosing comparison is fair.

## Scouting Classification

- `Plausible`: Supported by species PK, exposure-response logic, ADA context, and formulation feasibility.
- `Hypothesis`: Supported by partial PK or engineering rationale but not fully connected to dose.
- `Weak`: Based on half-life alone without exposure-response, ADA, or dose feasibility.
- `Not assessable`: Underlying PK/ADA data unavailable.

## CDA Gate Questions

- What exposure threshold supports the proposed dosing interval?
- What human dose and route are proposed?
- What is the expected injection volume or administration burden?
- Does tox support the projected exposure?
- What would be the fallback dosing interval if Q3M fails?
