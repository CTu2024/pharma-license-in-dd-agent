# CMC Readiness Review Skill

## Use When

Assessing whether the CMC package can support the asset's current stage, next clinical milestone, filing, launch supply, or transaction timeline.

## Review Method

1. Identify asset modality, dosage form, route, current phase, manufacturing scale, next milestone, and required supply use.
2. Compare the provided CMC package against stage-specific expectations for process, formulation, analytical, stability, specifications, and control strategy.
3. Review process maturity: batch history, yield, critical process parameters, process changes, comparability, deviations, and scale-up plan.
4. Review analytical readiness: method qualification or validation status, reference standards, potency assay, impurity methods, release testing, and specifications.
5. Review formulation and stability: dosage form, container closure, storage condition, shelf-life claim, forced degradation, and long-term stability coverage.
6. Review manufacturing readiness: facility, CDMO/vendor dependence, tech transfer, raw materials, capacity, GMP status, and clinical or commercial supply assumptions.
7. Translate CMC gaps into timeline, cost, regulatory, supply, valuation, and deal-structure implications.

## Output Fields

- CMC claim or requirement
- CMC area: process / analytical / formulation / stability / control strategy / supply / quality
- Company-provided evidence
- Requirement status: met / partially met / not met / not assessable
- Risk to next milestone
- Timeline or cost implication
- Specific company request

## Stage Readiness Ratings

- `Ready`: Evidence appears sufficient for the stated next milestone, subject to expert confirmation.
- `Ready with conditions`: Gaps exist but appear addressable without changing the core plan.
- `At risk`: Gaps could delay the next milestone, increase cost, or require material remediation.
- `Not assessable`: Primary CMC evidence is missing or too summary-level to judge.

## References

- Read `references/stage_requirements.md` when comparing the CMC package against preclinical, Phase 1, Phase 2, Phase 3, registration, or commercial expectations.
- Read `references/red_flags_and_scoring.md` when rating CMC readiness, identifying milestone risk, or translating CMC findings into deal terms.

## Quality Boundary

Do not treat a CMC summary as evidence of readiness when primary support is missing. Do not provide final GMP, quality release, process validation, or regulatory filing conclusions; escalate those to CMC, quality, and regulatory experts.
