# CMC Red Flags And Scoring

Use this reference when translating CMC findings into milestone and deal risk.

## High-Priority Red Flags

- CMC evidence is limited to a summary deck.
- Next clinical phase depends on unproven scale-up or site transfer.
- Long-term stability does not cover planned trial duration or storage condition.
- Potency or critical analytical method is not qualified, validated, or fit for purpose.
- Specifications are provisional without justification.
- Major process change lacks comparability strategy.
- Batch failures, low yield, deviations, OOS/OOT, or CAPA history are undisclosed.
- Single-source CDMO, raw material, device, or critical component risk is unresolved.
- CMC package assumes commercial readiness while still at clinical process maturity.
- Biologic, cell, gene, or complex modality lacks clear control strategy.

## CMC Readiness Scoring

Score each CMC area:

| Score | Meaning | Typical Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 3 | Ready | Primary CMC documents support next milestone; gaps are routine |
| 2 | Ready with conditions | Gaps are identifiable and likely addressable without changing the plan |
| 1 | At risk | Gaps may delay next milestone, increase cost, or require remediation |
| 0 | Not assessable | Primary CMC evidence is missing or summary-only |

Areas to score:

- Process maturity.
- Analytical readiness.
- Formulation and stability.
- Specifications and control strategy.
- Scale-up and comparability.
- Supply chain and vendor readiness.
- Quality/GMP readiness.

## Deal Implication Rules

- Any score 0 in a material area should trigger `Request More Data` or a gating condition.
- Any score 1 for Phase 3 or filing readiness should trigger timeline and cost downside scenarios.
- Scale-up, comparability, or stability uncertainty should influence milestones and conditions precedent.
- Commercial supply claims should not affect valuation unless supported by primary CMC and quality evidence.
