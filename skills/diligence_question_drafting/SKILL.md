# Diligence Question Drafting Skill

Use this skill after risks or gaps have been identified.

## Question Quality Standard

Good diligence questions are:

- Specific enough that the seller can answer or provide a document.
- Linked to a decision, risk, or assumption.
- Neutral in tone.
- Not compound unless the parts are inseparable.
- Assigned to a workstream and owner.

## Question Types

- `Document request`: Ask for a named document, dataset, report, or correspondence.
- `Clarification`: Resolve an inconsistency or ambiguous claim.
- `Expert call`: Prepare a question for KOL, regulator-facing, CMC, legal, or technical expert discussion.
- `Internal decision`: Ask the deal team to set assumptions, thresholds, or risk appetite.

## Output Fields

- Question ID
- Workstream
- Question
- Why it matters
- Related risk or gap ID
- Recipient
- Priority
- Status

## Style

Use concise professional language. Avoid accusatory phrasing. Ask for evidence rather than reassurance.
