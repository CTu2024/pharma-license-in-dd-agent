# Regulatory Pathway Requirements

Use this reference when checking whether the regulatory path is plausible.

## Core Documents

Request and review:

- Original agency meeting minutes and correspondence.
- Briefing books, written responses, information requests, and action trackers.
- IND/CTA status and major amendments.
- Designation documents: orphan, fast track, breakthrough, PRIME, RMAT, accelerated pathways, or local equivalents.
- Clinical development plan and proposed filing strategy.
- Relevant labels, review documents, and precedent approvals.
- Pediatric, special population, DDI, QT, CMC, and nonclinical requirements when relevant.

## Phase-Specific Focus

### Before Phase 2

- Is the endpoint acceptable for signal finding?
- Are safety, dose, and population assumptions sufficient for Phase 2?
- Has the agency flagged nonclinical, CMC, or clinical hold issues?

### Before Phase 3

- Has the agency aligned on population, endpoint, comparator, duration, statistical plan, and safety database?
- Are regional differences material?
- Are CMC and nonclinical packages sufficient to support Phase 3?
- Are accelerated or conditional pathways realistic, or only aspirational?

### Registration

- Are pivotal studies adequate and well controlled?
- Is the proposed label supported by evidence?
- Are integrated safety, CMC, inspection, pediatric, and post-marketing requirements addressed?
- Are advisory committee or risk-management issues likely?

## Precedent Review

For each precedent, capture:

- Product, mechanism, indication, line of therapy, and region.
- Endpoint, comparator, effect size, safety database, and label claim.
- Approval basis and major review issues.
- Similarities and differences from the target asset.
- Whether the precedent supports, weakens, or does not apply to the proposed path.
