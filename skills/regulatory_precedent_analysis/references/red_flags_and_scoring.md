# Regulatory Red Flags And Scoring

Use this reference when rating regulatory path confidence.

## High-Priority Red Flags

- Only company-prepared agency summaries are available.
- Agency minutes contain unresolved objections or action items.
- Proposed Phase 3 endpoint differs from accepted precedent without justification.
- Patient population or comparator does not match current standard of care.
- Safety database appears too small for chronic use or expected label.
- CMC readiness is assumed despite unresolved comparability, stability, or control strategy issues.
- Accelerated pathway claim lacks surrogate endpoint validation or agency support.
- Regional filing plan ignores known FDA/EMA/NMPA/PMDA differences.
- Required pediatric, special population, DDI, QT, or long-term safety plans are missing.
- Label or claims strategy exceeds available evidence.

## Regulatory Confidence Scoring

| Score | Meaning | Typical Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 3 | Plausible | Original agency feedback and precedent support the path |
| 2 | Plausible with conditions | Path is credible but depends on resolving defined gaps |
| 1 | At risk | Material agency, endpoint, safety, CMC, or precedent issues could change plan |
| 0 | Not assessable | Original agency records or key precedent comparison missing |

## Conclusion Rules

- `Supportive`: Score 3 for core path and no unresolved agency objection.
- `Supportive With Conditions`: Score 2 with clear, addressable agency or package gaps.
- `Mixed`: Multiple score 1-2 issues with meaningful timeline, label, or study-design risk.
- `Not Supportive`: Precedent or agency feedback contradicts proposed path.
- `Not Assessable`: Agency documents or necessary precedent evidence are missing.

## Required Regulatory Requests

- Original meeting minutes, correspondence, briefing books, written responses.
- Agency action tracker and unresolved commitments.
- Precedent analysis supporting endpoint, population, comparator, and filing path.
- Regional regulatory strategy.
- Label hypothesis and evidence-to-claim mapping.
