# Biostatistical Robustness Skill

## Use When

Clinical claims depend on study design, analysis quality, or interpretation of statistical evidence.

## Review Method

1. Identify objectives, endpoints, estimands, population, sample size, and analysis sets.
2. Check prespecification: protocol, amendments, SAP, endpoint hierarchy, multiplicity, and interim rules.
3. Review missing data, protocol deviations, subgroup analyses, and sensitivity analyses.
4. Distinguish nominal, exploratory, post hoc, and confirmatory findings.
5. Rate whether each clinical claim is statistically reliable.

## Output Fields

- Claim
- Statistical support
- Robustness concern
- Missing analysis or document
- Impact on decision
- Company request

## References

- Read `references/study_design_checklist.md` when reviewing protocol, SAP, endpoint hierarchy, estimands, sample size, or study design adequacy.
- Read `references/red_flags_and_scoring.md` when rating robustness, identifying statistical red flags, or drafting statistical document and analysis requests.

## Quality Boundary

Do not treat post hoc, underpowered, unadjusted, or selectively reported findings as confirmatory.
