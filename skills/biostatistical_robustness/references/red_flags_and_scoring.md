# Biostatistical Red Flags And Scoring

Use this reference when rating statistical robustness.

## High-Priority Red Flags

- SAP unavailable or finalized after unblinding.
- Endpoint hierarchy, multiplicity control, or alpha allocation missing.
- Primary endpoint failed but secondary or subgroup results are emphasized.
- Subgroup claim lacks interaction test or prespecification.
- Missing data are imbalanced or handled only with favorable assumptions.
- Analysis population changes are unexplained.
- Interim analysis, stopping rule, or sample size re-estimation is unclear.
- Baseline imbalance could bias outcome.
- P-values are reported without confidence intervals or effect-size context.
- Trial is underpowered for the claim being made.
- Multiple doses, endpoints, or timepoints are mined without adjustment.

## Robustness Scoring

| Score | Meaning | Typical Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 3 | Robust | Prespecified, adequately powered, multiplicity-controlled, consistent, sensitivity-supported |
| 2 | Moderately robust | Directionally reliable with defined limitations |
| 1 | Weak | Exploratory, underpowered, post hoc, missing-data-sensitive, or selectively reported |
| 0 | Not assessable | SAP, protocol, or key analyses missing |

## Claim-Level Rating Rules

- Confirmatory claims require score 3.
- Phase 2 proof-of-concept claims may be acceptable at score 2 if limitations are explicit.
- Subgroup claims cannot exceed score 1 unless prespecified, powered or interaction-supported, and clinically plausible.
- Durability claims require appropriate follow-up and missing-data handling.
- Safety comparisons require adequate exposure and event capture; avoid overprecision with sparse events.

## Required Statistical Requests

- SAP and amendments with dates.
- Endpoint hierarchy, multiplicity plan, and alpha allocation.
- Sample size assumptions and observed variance/effect size.
- Missing data methods and sensitivity analyses.
- Subgroup analysis plan and interaction tests.
- Tables, figures, listings, and datasets.
