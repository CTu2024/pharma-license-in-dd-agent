# License-In Due Diligence Triage Memo

## Opportunity Snapshot

- Asset: XYZ-204
- Licensor: Not provided
- Modality: Subcutaneous biologic
- Indication: Moderate-to-severe inflammatory disease after inadequate response to first-line therapy
- Stage: Phase 2 completed; regional license sought before Phase 3
- Geography: Regional license, territory not specified
- Proposed rights: Incomplete; proposed regional license with upfront, milestones, and tiered royalties
- Next decision milestone: Decide whether to proceed toward term-sheet negotiation, pause, renegotiate economics, or reject

## Recommendation

Recommendation: `Renegotiate`

Rationale:

XYZ-204 appears potentially diligence-worthy because the licensor reports a clinically meaningful Phase 2 signal and differentiated monthly dosing profile. However, the current package is too summary-heavy to support the proposed economics as presented. The largest decision risks are full verification of Phase 2 clinical and statistical robustness, Phase 3 regulatory alignment, CMC scale-up from 500L to 2000L, incomplete stability, IP encumbrance from licensed background IP, and unsupported premium pricing assumptions.

The team should not reject solely on the current information, but should avoid moving forward on the licensor's proposed economics until primary documents and rights constraints are reviewed. A staged structure, lower upfront, data-driven milestones, CMC/regulatory conditions, and access-sensitive economics should be considered.

This memo integrates discipline-level reports in `workstream_reports/`. It is a decision summary, not a replacement for workstream analysis.

## Conditions To Change Recommendation

- Change to `Pursue` if full Phase 2 data, agency minutes, CMC scale-up plan, IP documents, and payer evidence support the licensor's claims and economics.
- Change to `Pause` if the licensor cannot provide primary clinical, regulatory, CMC, IP, and rights documents before term-sheet negotiation.
- Change to `Reject` if Phase 2 robustness is weak, agency feedback requires major redesign, IP rights are materially encumbered, or CMC scale-up is not feasible.
- Maintain `Renegotiate` if the asset remains attractive but risk-adjusted economics require lower upfront, staged milestones, option structure, or CMC/regulatory conditions.

## Escalations For Human Expert Review

- Legal: Review background IP agreement, rights matrix, FTO, sublicensing, assignment, and change-of-control terms.
- Medical: Review full CSR, safety narratives, and benefit-risk once provided.
- Regulatory: Review original agency correspondence and Phase 3 alignment.
- CMC / Quality: Review comparability, scale-up, analytical validation, and long-term stability.
- Finance: Re-run valuation with downside scenarios for delayed Phase 3 start, lower price, lower POS, and royalty stack.
