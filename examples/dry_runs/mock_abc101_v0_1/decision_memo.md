## Recommendation

Recommendation: `Request More Data`

Rationale:

ABC-101 may merit continued diligence, but the current package is not sufficient to support a confident pursue or reject recommendation. The key licensor claims on target engagement, early clinical activity, and clean safety profile are supported only by an investor presentation and Phase 1b synopsis, not by the full clinical study report, protocol, statistical analysis plan, or patient-level safety tables. CMC and regulatory readiness also depend on summary documents rather than full analytical validation data and original agency correspondence.

The deal team should request primary source documents before valuation, term-sheet negotiation, or governance escalation.

## Conditions To Change Recommendation

- Change to `Pursue` if primary clinical, CMC, target biology, and regulatory documents confirm the summary claims without material new risks.
- Change to `Pause` if the licensor cannot provide full CSR, SAP, original agency correspondence, or CMC validation package in a reasonable diligence window.
- Change to `Reject` if primary documents show weak efficacy, unacceptable safety signal, non-remediable CMC gaps, or agency feedback requiring a substantially larger development program than represented.
- Change to `Renegotiate` if the asset remains attractive but added development, CMC, or regulatory risk materially reduces value.

## Escalations For Human Expert Review

- Legal: Confirm proposed rights, encumbrances, sublicensing rights, and any confidentiality limits before deeper diligence.
- Medical: Review full CSR and patient-level safety data when received.
- Regulatory: Review original agency correspondence and assess next-milestone requirements.
- CMC / Quality: Review analytical validation, stability, batch history, and quality system evidence.
- Finance: Defer valuation until primary clinical, CMC, and regulatory claims are verified.
