# Scouting Triage Memo: Kv1.3 Asset Potential

## Opportunity Snapshot

- Asset: BPX-H008 / DPT0218-like gut-selective Kv1.3 program; exact asset identity and sponsor require confirmation
- Target / pathway: Kv1.3 / KCNA3 potassium channel; effector-memory T-cell calcium signaling and immune activation
- Modality: Presumed oral small-molecule Kv1.3 inhibitor for the asset under review; broader target field includes topical small molecules, peptides, antibodies, small proteins, and bispecific/fusion approaches
- Stage: Scouting / pre-CDA; target field is mostly preclinical to early clinical, with no approved Kv1.3 products
- Most relevant indications: For the asset, IBD / gut inflammation appears most logical if gut-selective exposure is real. For the target overall, skin immune inflammation has more visible clinical precedent.
- Source type: User-provided target research report generated 2026-07-03, prior workspace scouting memo, and spot checks of public trial/company pages on 2026-07-08
- Evidence maturity: Target-level evidence is medium. Asset-specific evidence is low to medium because primary BPX-H008 reports, potency-adjusted exposure, safety margins, and transaction rights are not yet available.

## Scouting Recommendation

Recommendation: `Request Non-Confidential Follow-Up`

Kv1.3 is a legitimate immune-inflammation target with enough biology, structural druggability, early human precedent, and BD activity to justify continued scouting. The target is not merely speculative. It has a coherent mechanism in effector-memory T cells, multiple human structural templates, and early clinical signals from dalazatide in psoriasis.

The specific asset should not move directly to CDA yet. The gut-selective oral thesis could be attractive for IBD, but the current package does not prove the key value-driving claims: free intestinal exposure over human cellular IC50, lamina propria immune-cell target engagement, family ion-channel selectivity, chronic safety window, human dose feasibility, or asset-specific differentiation versus Lilly/DESRES, Zealand, and YR programs.

## Walk-Away Signals

- The company cannot clearly identify the asset, its relationship to DPT0218 / DP10020069, or its ownership.
- No final primary reports exist for potency, selectivity, tissue PK, efficacy, and initial safety.
- Gut selectivity is based only on total tissue levels, without free exposure or immune-cell target-engagement rationale.
- Selectivity versus Kv1 family members, hERG, Nav, and Cav is weak or not tested.
- Management cannot credibly explain differentiation from Lilly/DESRES and other systemic Kv1.3 programs.
- Lead indication remains vague after follow-up.
- Rights, field, territory, or patent control are unclear.
- Seller seeks CDA or valuation discussion before providing a basic non-confidential data inventory.

## Recommended Next Action

Send the non-confidential follow-up request first. If the seller can confirm asset identity, rights, final report availability, human Kv1.3 potency/selectivity, credible gut free-exposure logic, and a clear IBD lead indication, schedule a 30-45 minute intro call. Proceed to CDA only if that call confirms a coherent TPP, real primary reports, compatible rights, and an internal scientific owner.
