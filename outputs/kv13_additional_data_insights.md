# Kv1.3 / BPX-H008 Additional Data Insights

## Executive Read

The new PDF and BioPartnersX deck materially improve the asset-specific story. The prior memo treated the gut-selective thesis as plausible but under-supported; the new files provide direct rat tissue concentration data, lamina propria concentration-time plots, and a rat oxazolone colitis DAI efficacy figure. This moves the asset from a mostly target-level opportunity to a more coherent preclinical asset thesis.

The recommendation is upgraded in practical terms from "send follow-up and wait" to: `Schedule an intro call with targeted non-confidential follow-up before CDA`. I still would not proceed directly to CDA because the package remains slide-level, uses multiple asset names, and does not include primary reports, full methods, raw data, tox, selectivity, human dose projection, IP, or rights.

## Revised Asset Thesis

The most attractive version of the story is now:

BPX-H008 / DPT0218-like compound is an oral, gut-restricted Kv1.3 inhibitor intended to suppress pathogenic effector-memory T cells in IBD while avoiding systemic exposure liabilities seen with broader immunosuppressants. The strongest asset-specific evidence is the combination of high intestinal/lamina propria exposure, low plasma and off-target tissue exposure, and rat colitis efficacy versus relevant oral IBD comparators.

This is a better thesis than "generic systemic Kv1.3 inhibition." It is especially more compelling than trying to compete first in systemic psoriasis, where mature IL-17/IL-23/TYK2/JAK options create a high efficacy bar.

## Key Implications For BD

1. The asset deserves a live intro call.
   The additional data are sufficiently specific and positive to justify a 30-45 minute scientific/BD intro call.

2. Do not sign CDA yet.
   Before CDA, ask for a non-confidential data inventory and a focused PK/PD/selectivity appendix. The company should be able to provide this without disclosing structures or raw reports.

3. Lead indication should be UC first unless they have Crohn's-specific data.
   UC has clearer colonic mucosal biology, more direct tissue expression rationale, and a more straightforward early PoC path than Crohn's. Crohn's can remain a second IBD expansion route.

4. The TPP should be "oral gut-restricted immune modulator," not "JAK-like efficacy."
   The sponsor's "safer than JAK with comparable efficacy" framing is ambitious. A more credible early BD framing is: oral, local gut exposure, low systemic exposure, active in colitis model, potentially useful in patients needing a safer chronic oral immune modulator.

5. The most important next proof is exposure-response, not another single efficacy plot.
   The story becomes much stronger if they can link human Kv1.3/TEM functional potency, unbound lamina propria exposure, cytokine suppression, DAI, colon length, and histology in one exposure-response package.

## Updated Follow-Up Questions

| Question | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Are BPX-H008, DPT0218, and DP10020069 the same molecule, backup compounds, or related scaffolds? | Clarifies what asset is actually available |
| What is the human TEM-cell functional IC50, and how was it measured? | Anchors the lamina propria exposure claim |
| What are unbound fractions in plasma, colon tissue, ileum tissue, and lamina propria cell preparations? | Converts total concentrations into pharmacologically meaningful exposure |
| Please provide the rat and beagle PK tables behind the "multiple species" claim | Confirms translation beyond a single rat experiment |
| What is the full oxazolone study design: n, randomization, blinding, dosing start, disease induction, endpoints, and statistics? | Tests robustness of the efficacy signal |
| Do colon length, histopathology, body weight, cytokines, and fecal/inflammatory biomarkers align with the DAI improvement? | Determines whether this is symptom-like improvement or tissue-level disease modification |
| What are the hERG, Kv1.1, Kv1.5, Kv7, Nav, Cav, CNS, cardiac, and respiratory safety pharmacology results? | Core ion-channel safety screen |
| What tox studies are complete versus planned, and what exposure multiples were achieved? | Determines whether 2027 IND timing is credible |
| What is the intended first-in-human dose projection and expected human colon/ileum free exposure? | Tests clinical feasibility |
| What rights are available and what transaction structure is sought? | Determines whether this is actionable for BD |

## Revised Recommendation

Recommendation: `Intro Call` with a targeted non-confidential pre-read request.

Proceed to CDA only if the intro call confirms:

- BPX-H008 identity and ownership are clear.
- Full primary reports exist for potency, selectivity, PK, lamina propria exposure, efficacy, and initial safety.
- The gut free-exposure margin over human TEM-cell functional IC50 is credible.
- Efficacy is supported by more than DAI, including histology and colon-length endpoints.
- Beagle or other non-rodent exposure supports human translation.
- IND-enabling studies are realistically on track for a 2027 FDA/NMPA submission.
- Rights and deal structure are compatible with our mandate.

## Bottom Line

The new data make this a more serious scouting opportunity. The strongest reason to keep going is the emerging alignment between mechanism, site of disease, gut-localized exposure, lamina propria coverage, and rat colitis efficacy. The main reason to stay disciplined is that the package is still a sponsor slide story rather than a diligence-grade data room. This is now worth an intro call, but not yet worth CDA without a focused non-confidential data inventory and PK/PD/selectivity clarification.
