# Scouting Triage Memo: BPX-H008 / DPT0218-Like Gut-Selective Kv1.3 Program

## Opportunity Snapshot

- Company: BioPartnersX / BPX, based on user-provided shorthand; exact sponsor/entity to confirm
- Asset: BPX-H008, with DPT0218 / DP10020069 used as apparent comparator or related scaffold in source material
- Target / pathway: Kv1.3 potassium channel; immune-cell modulation, especially memory T cells / tissue-resident mucosal immune cells
- Modality: Oral small molecule Kv1.3 inhibitor
- Stage: Preclinical or early translational scouting stage for BPX-H008; human clinical read-across available from Lilly LY3972406 psoriasis study
- Indications: Most plausible scouting thesis is IBD / intestinal inflammation; psoriasis is relevant as negative human target-class read-across
- Proposed transaction / rights: Unknown
- Source type: Mixed pre-CDA scouting package: user-provided clinical-trial note, rat tissue-distribution summary, and presentation image
- Source date: Not provided by source package; memo prepared 2026-07-01
- Evidence maturity: Low to medium. There is nonclinical tissue-distribution and animal efficacy support, plus external human clinical read-across, but no BPX-H008 human PK/PD, tox, CMC, IP, or rights package provided.

## Scouting Recommendation

Recommendation: `Request Non-Confidential Follow-Up`

Rationale:

The opportunity is interesting enough to keep active because the package suggests a coherent gut-selective Kv1.3 strategy: high ileum/colon exposure, low plasma and off-target tissue exposure, and rat colitis-model efficacy. This could be attractive if the intended lead indication is IBD and if local lamina propria exposure is sufficient for durable target engagement while limiting systemic safety exposure.

However, a direct move to CDA looks premature from the current package. Lilly's LY3972406 psoriasis result is a cautionary human signal for the target or exposure hypothesis: PASI-75 was only 18.8% versus 5.9% placebo in a small posted dataset, and the dose, potency, and target-coverage assumptions are redacted. The key scouting question is whether BPX-H008 is deliberately differentiated from LY3972406 by gut-restricted distribution and indication choice, or whether the Lilly result reflects a broader Kv1.3 efficacy limitation.

## Walk-Away Signals

- BPX-H008 is not clearly distinct from Lilly LY3972406 or cannot explain why the negative psoriasis result does not matter.
- No full primary reports exist for potency, selectivity, tissue PK, in vivo efficacy, and initial safety pharmacology.
- Gut exposure is based only on total tissue concentration, with no free-exposure or target-engagement rationale.
- Low plasma exposure prevents systemic safety risk but also fails to achieve sustained local gut pharmacology.
- Lead indication remains vague after follow-up.
- IP ownership, composition coverage, or freedom-to-operate position is unclear.
- Seller will not provide a data inventory or transaction-rights summary before CDA.

## Recommended Next Action

- Send a non-confidential follow-up request focused on asset identity, lead indication, pharmacology potency/selectivity, gut free-exposure rationale, Lilly LY3972406 differentiation, data inventory, IP family summary, and transaction rights.
- If the response confirms that full primary reports exist and the gut-selective thesis is deliberately differentiated from LY3972406, schedule a 30-45 minute intro call using the framework agenda.
- Move to CDA only if the intro-call response confirms a coherent IBD lead indication, available rights, credible primary reports, and an internal R&D owner.
